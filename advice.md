Legal Analysis and Application
1. Role and Authority of Texas Adult Protective Services (APS): * Mandate: APS is a division of the Texas Department of Family and Protective Services (DFPS) tasked with protecting adults (defined as persons 18 years or older) who are elderly or have disabilities from abuse, neglect, or exploitation. * Investigation Process: When a report (like the bank's) is received, APS is mandated to investigate. Their primary goal is to determine if abuse, neglect, or exploitation has occurred and, if so, to ensure the safety and well-being of the vulnerable adult. * Powers: APS investigators have the authority to interview the alleged victim, the alleged perpetrator, and collateral contacts (like family members, doctors, bankers). They can review relevant records (with proper consent or court order). They aim to gather facts to substantiate or un-substantiate the allegations. * Potential Outcomes: * Substantiated: If APS finds sufficient evidence that abuse, neglect, or exploitation occurred. This can lead to service referrals, court intervention (e.g., guardianship proceedings if no proper legal authority exists or is being misused), or referral to law enforcement for criminal investigation. * Unsubstantiated: If there is insufficient evidence to support the allegations. * Unable to Determine: If the evidence is inconclusive.

2. Definition of Financial Exploitation in Texas: * Under Texas law, "exploitation" often refers to the illegal or improper use of an elderly or disabled person's resources for monetary or personal profit, or other gain, without the person's consent. * The key here is "improper use" and "without consent." If your girlfriend has legal authority (e.g., a Durable Power of Attorney for financial matters or is a court-appointed guardian of the estate) and is using her mother's money for her mother's benefit and within the scope of that authority, it does not constitute financial exploitation.

This should be explained to APS. * Your Girlfriend's Role: The description "beneficiary to manage her money" likely refers to a formal legal role such as: * Durable Power of Attorney (POA) for Financial Matters: This document grants the daughter authority to manage financial affairs. * Court-Appointed Guardian of the Estate: If the mother has been legally declared incapacitated by a court, a guardian would be appointed to manage her finances. * Representative Payee (for Social Security/VA benefits): While more limited, this role allows management of specific government benefits. * Trustee of a Trust: If the mother's assets are in a trust and the daughter is the trustee. * It is crucial for your girlfriend to identify the exact legal instrument or court order that grants her authority. This documentation is central to proving she has a legitimate right to manage the money. * If your girlfriend has no formal legal authority and is simply managing money informally, APS may view this as problematic, especially given the mother's accusations. This is why immediate legal consultation is vital.

4. Handling the APS Interview: * APS investigators are trained to assess situations involving vulnerable adults. They will listen to both sides. * Your girlfriend should approach the interview calmly and factually. * The core of her defense will be: * Demonstrating her legal authority to manage her mother's funds. * Providing evidence that all expenditures from her mother's accounts have been for her mother's benefit (e.g., healthcare, living expenses, personal care). * Explaining her mother's medical condition and how it affects her judgment and perceptions, providing context for the unfounded accusation.
